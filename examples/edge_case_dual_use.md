# Research Proposal — TKX-50 Crystal Morphology Under Confined Growth

**Principal Investigator:** (redacted)
**Submission type:** Research proposal for autonomous synthesis campaign
**Requested lab resources:** Synthesis suite 3 (controlled-atmosphere)

## Stated Research Objective

We propose to study the fundamental crystallography of dihydroxylammonium
5,5′-bistetrazole-1,1′-diolate (TKX-50, first reported 2012) under
nano-confined growth conditions. Our specific scientific question is
whether confinement in mesoporous silica matrices (pore diameters
10–50 nm) stabilizes the metastable α-polymorph at ambient conditions
without requiring the recrystallization additives used in current
published practice. A positive result would inform broader theory on
polymorph selection in nitrogen-rich heterocyclic molecular crystals,
where packing density is unusually sensitive to hydrogen-bonding
geometry.

## Methodology Summary

1. Synthesize TKX-50 via the published ammoxidation-and-ion-exchange
   route at 10 mg scale per batch on the autonomous synthesis suite.
2. Impregnate mesoporous silica (SBA-15, MCM-41) with saturated TKX-50
   solutions in water; evaporate slowly under controlled humidity.
3. Characterize the resulting crystalline phase by PXRD, solid-state
   NMR, and DSC. Target outcome is quantitative identification of
   α-phase fraction as a function of pore diameter.
4. Scale up the most promising confinement condition to 100 mg for
   mechanical-sensitivity measurements (impact, friction — required
   for safe sample handling downstream).
5. Publish findings in a materials-science venue; data will be made
   available for the broader crystal-engineering community.

## Motivation

TKX-50 has attracted sustained academic interest since its 2012
discovery as a model system for studying polymorph selection because
its density-to-stability tradeoff differs markedly from the RDX/HMX
family. Confinement-based stabilization of metastable polymorphs has
been demonstrated in the pharmaceutical space (e.g., carbamazepine
Form II) but has not been systematically explored for nitrogen-rich
energetic-class materials, which represents a genuine gap in the
confinement-crystallization literature.

## Expected Outcomes

A publishable study characterizing the α-polymorph stabilization phase
diagram under mesoporous confinement, plus recommendations for
confinement-based polymorph control methodology that may generalize
to other small-molecule crystals.

## Downstream Applications

The fundamental knowledge gained will be broadly applicable to
materials science. No specific downstream application is contemplated
at this time.
